# Introduction

Much of the world experienced European colonialism. Yet, its consequences were not the same everywhere.

Today, the descendants of pre-colonial peoples remain the overwhelming majority in much of Asia and Africa. In the Americas, however, the descendants of the peoples who inhabited the continents before European arrival became minorities in many regions and came to be known collectively as "Indigenous peoples."

Why did history unfold so differently in the Americas?

Historians often point to several factors: the peoples of the Americas lacked many of the domesticated animals and technologies common in Eurasia, such as horses and ironworking, and were devastated by Eurasian diseases to which they had no immunity.

But what if these circumstances had been different?

There was, perhaps, one historical event that could have changed everything—the arrival of the Vikings in North America.

Around the year 1000, Norse explorers reached what was likely Newfoundland and named it Vinland. In our timeline, their settlement was short-lived and was abandoned after only a few years due to conflict with the native inhabitants and the difficulties of maintaining contact with Greenland and Europe.

This wiki explores an alternate history in which the Norse were more committed to settling North America than they were in reality. Bringing with them ironworking, domesticated animals, and Eurasian diseases, they introduced these influences to the peoples of the Americas nearly five centuries before the arrival of Spain.

This is not a world in which European colonialism never happened. Even in Eurasia, societies possessing iron, horses, and immunity to Old World diseases were subjected to colonial rule. There is little reason to believe that the peoples of the Americas would have been entirely exempt from it.

Instead, this project asks a different question:

What if the peoples, nations, cultures, and civilizations of the Americas survived into the modern age to a far greater extent than they did in our own history?

The Meeting of Two Worlds is an attempt to imagine that world—a plausible alternate history in which American and Eurasian civilizations met, influenced one another, and together shaped the course of modern history.
